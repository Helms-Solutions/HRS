Medicare Options

Understand the major paths before you choose

Medicare choices can affect your costs, doctor access, prescriptions, and flexibility. Helms helps explain the tradeoffs in plain language.

01

Medicare Supplement

Often called Medigap, these plans help pay some of the costs Original Medicare does not cover.

  • Broad provider access
  • Predictable cost structure
  • Popular options include Plan G and Plan N
  • Already on Plan F? Review whether lower-premium options may be available while keeping coverage that still fits your needs.
02

Medicare Advantage

Private plans that combine Medicare benefits and may include extras depending on carrier and service area.

  • Low or $0 premium options may be available
  • Network and prior authorization rules may apply
  • May include dental, vision, hearing, or OTC benefits
03

Part D Prescription Plans

Prescription coverage should be reviewed around your actual medications, dosage, and preferred pharmacy.

  • Drug formularies and tiers
  • Preferred pharmacy pricing
  • Annual review as plans and medications change

Extra Protection

Medicare Advantage copays and extra coverage deserve a closer look

Some Medicare Advantage plans can include higher copays for hospital stays, outpatient care, specialist visits, or major health events. Extra benefits may also vary by plan, carrier, service area, and year, so it is important to understand what is guaranteed, what can change, and where a separate policy may help.

01

Hospital Indemnity

Hospital indemnity coverage may help pay cash benefits after a covered hospital stay, which can help offset inpatient copays or related expenses.

  • Review hospital copays
  • Compare benefit amounts
  • Match coverage to your plan design
02

Dental Coverage

Some plans include dental benefits, but covered services, annual maximums, provider access, and benefit amounts can vary. Separate dental coverage may help fill gaps.

  • Check annual maximums
  • Review dentures, crowns, and implants
  • Compare network and provider rules
03

Critical Illness & Cancer

Critical illness or cancer coverage may provide cash benefits after a covered diagnosis, helping with treatment-related costs, travel, household bills, or other needs.

  • Review cancer and heart/stroke exposure
  • Understand benefit triggers
  • Coordinate coverage with your Medicare plan
